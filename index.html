On keeping a record
no one asked for

There is a particular kind of writing that only works when it is unsigned. Not because the words are dangerous, but because a name turns a thought into a claim, and a claim into something to defend. Anonymous, the sentence is allowed to be wrong tomorrow.

So this is a place for thinking out loud with the volume turned down. No metrics on the wall, no followers to keep warm, no version of myself to maintain. Just the latest idea, given the whole room, and the older ones filed quietly to the side.

If you found this, you found it the slow way — a link passed sideways, a margin note in someone else's day. That feels like the right speed. Stay as long as the thought is useful, and no longer.
